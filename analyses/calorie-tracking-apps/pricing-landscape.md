# Pricing Landscape: Calorie/Food Tracking Apps

*Generated: March 10, 2026*

## Market Pricing Overview

- **Dominant value metric:** Feature access (barcode scanning, ad removal, advanced insights, meal planning) gated behind subscriptions
- **Price range:** $38.99/yr (FatSecret) to $209/yr (Noom Weight) for consumer tiers
- **Median price point:** ~$50/yr for standard premium tier
- **Free tier prevalence:** 7 of 8 major competitors offer free (MacroFactor is the exception)
- **Lifetime option:** Only Lose It! ($189.99 one-time)

**Universal switching cost:** No app offers meaningful data portability. Users cannot export food logs to competing apps. Years of custom foods, recipes, and logs are effectively locked in. Building easy data import FROM competitors is a powerful acquisition tool.

## Pricing Whitespace

### 1. Mid-Market Coaching ($100-150/yr)
There's a massive gap between pure tracking apps (~$50/yr) and Noom's coaching ($209+/yr). An app offering adaptive coaching (MacroFactor-style algorithm) at ~$100-150/yr fills this gap. Users want more than passive tracking but don't want to pay $17+/mo for Noom's generic coaching.

### 2. Family/Team Plans
Almost no competitor offers family pricing. Households share meals — a family plan at $69-99/yr (vs. $50/yr per individual) could capture household budget and increase retention through social accountability.

### 3. Usage-Based Pricing
No competitor charges per-scan or per-meal. A pay-per-use model could serve intermittent users who don't want subscriptions but would pay $0.10-0.25 per AI meal scan. Reduces commitment friction.

### 4. Professional/B2B Tier Expansion
Only Cronometer has a meaningful pro tier ($39.99/mo + $2.50/client). Dietitians, coaches, and trainers need client management tools. The professional nutrition software market is underserved by consumer app companies.

### 5. Employer/Insurance Channel
Noom is pricing B2B at premium levels. An affordable per-employee nutrition tracking benefit ($3-5/employee/month) could undercut Noom's corporate wellness positioning while reaching the $94B market.

## Recommendations

- **If competing on price:** Position at $29-39/yr (undercutting MFP by 50%+) with FatSecret-level free tier generosity. Win on value perception: "everything MFP charges $80 for, we include at half the price."
- **If competing on value:** Position at $70-100/yr with adaptive coaching and verified database. Fill the gap between passive tracking ($50/yr) and Noom coaching ($200+/yr). MacroFactor proves users will pay $72/yr for intelligence.
- **If competing on model:** Try usage-based pricing ($0.10-0.25 per AI scan) or lifetime pricing (Lose It! is the only competitor doing this). Alternatively, B2B-first model licensing nutrition data API (FatSecret's approach) to fund a free consumer app.

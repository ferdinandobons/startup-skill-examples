# Battle Card: Cronometer

*Last updated: March 10, 2026*

## At a Glance
- **What they do:** Precision nutrition tracker with verified food data and 84-nutrient micronutrient tracking
- **Founded:** 2011 | **Funding:** $47K (bootstrapped) | **Team:** ~45
- **Price:** Free / $49.99-59.88/yr (Gold) / $39.99/mo (Pro) | **Model:** Freemium + professional tier
- **Best for:** Data-driven users, biohackers, people with medical dietary needs, and nutrition professionals

## Their Strengths (be honest)
- **Gold standard for data accuracy** — every food entry verified by staff against lab-analyzed sources. No unverified crowdsourced data [Data]
- **Deepest micronutrient tracking (84 nutrients)** — no competitor comes close. Tracks vitamins, minerals, amino acids, fatty acids [Data]
- **Profitable bootstrapped business** — $5.1M revenue, growing without external funding. Proves the model works [Data]
- **Professional tier** — Cronometer Pro serves dietitians/nutritionists ($39.99/mo + $2.50/client). Only meaningful B2B nutrition tool besides Noom [Data]

## Their Weaknesses (your openings)
- **Aggressive ads destroy free experience:** "Ads now hijack the app for up to half a minute" — unskippable video ads appear mid-logging. Users more likely to uninstall than upgrade [Data — App Store, Cronometer Forums]
- **Overwhelming for casual users:** Tracks so much data it can feel intimidating. Steeper learning curve than MFP or Lose It! [Data — reviews]
- **Smaller food database:** Fewer restaurant and branded items than MFP (14M) or Lose It! (57M). Accuracy comes at the cost of coverage [Data]
- **Small team (45 people)** limits pace of feature development. Slow to ship AI features, mobile improvements [Data]

## How to Win Against Them
- **When they say "Most accurate database in the market,"** respond: "Accuracy is great, but Cronometer makes you work for it. Our verified database is just as trustworthy — wrapped in a UI that doesn't overwhelm you with 84 data points by default."
- **When they say "Professional-grade nutrition tracking,"** respond: "Professional complexity shouldn't mean 30-second unskippable ads. We give you verified accuracy without the hostile free experience."
- **Lead with:** Equivalent data accuracy with progressive disclosure UX — simple by default, detailed on demand

## When They Win Over You
- Biohackers and longevity enthusiasts who genuinely need 84-nutrient tracking
- Nutrition professionals who need Cronometer Pro for client management
- Users with specific medical conditions (diabetes, micronutrient deficiencies) who need maximum data granularity

## Their Customers' Top Complaint
"Ads now hijack the app for up to half a minute... more likely to uninstall the app than pay for the ad-free version" — App Store & Cronometer Forums, 2025
This matters because: Hostile ad monetization is actively driving uninstalls. Users who came for accuracy leave because the free experience is unbearable — and Gold at ~$55/yr feels like paying to remove abuse.

## Key Vulnerability
**Accuracy without accessibility.** Cronometer owns the "accuracy" niche but is trapped there. Casual users find it overwhelming. The aggressive ad monetization alienates potential converts. And the small team can't build the UX improvements needed to broaden appeal while maintaining the data quality that defines them.

## Churn Signals
- Ad hostility driving uninstalls (free users) — frequency: common
- Overwhelming data for non-power-users — frequency: occasional
- UI regressions in updates (dark mode, spacing) — frequency: occasional
- Missing restaurant/branded foods — frequency: occasional

## Watch For
- Glucose tracking integration (September 2025) signals a move toward diabetes/metabolic health niche.
- Whether they improve the free tier ad experience or continue hostile monetization.
- Potential acquisition target for a larger player wanting verified nutrition data.

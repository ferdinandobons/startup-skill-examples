# Battle Card: Noom

*Last updated: March 10, 2026*

## At a Glance
- **What they do:** Psychology-based weight management platform combining CBT coaching, food tracking, and GLP-1 medication programs
- **Founded:** 2008 | **Funding:** $650M+ (Silver Lake, Sequoia, Temasek) | **Team:** ~1,200
- **Price:** $209/yr (Weight, 12mo) to $279/mo (Med GLP-1) | **Model:** Commitment-based subscription + telehealth
- **Best for:** Users willing to invest heavily in behavioral weight loss coaching and/or GLP-1 medications

## Their Strengths (be honest)
- **Massive scale and funding:** $3.7B valuation, ~$1B ARR, and $650M+ raised. Can outspend every competitor on advertising ($21M+ on digital ads in a single month) [Data]
- **Unique positioning:** Psychology-first approach (CBT) is genuinely differentiated. No competitor offers behavioral coaching at this scale [Data]
- **GLP-1 medication pivot:** Noom Med reached $100M revenue run-rate within 4 months of launch. First mover in connecting calorie tracking + medication prescriptions [Data]
- **B2B/enterprise growth:** Highmark partnership reaching 2M eligible members. Castlight Health, Accolade, LillyDirect partnerships [Data]

## Their Weaknesses (your openings)
- **$56M billing lawsuit settlement** — autorenewal and cancellation practices. BBB received 1,200+ complaints. Users report being charged months after canceling [Data]
- **Generic coaching:** "Coaches often provide generic advice" and "automated responses." Users paying $17-42/mo expect personalized human coaching and feel deceived [Data — ConsumerAffairs, multiple platforms]
- **Weak food database:** Only 3M items (vs. MFP's 14M, Lose It!'s 57M). "Database lacks many common foods and calorie counts can be off" [Data]
- **Extreme price premium:** At $17.42/mo (annual), Noom costs 3-5x more than every pure tracking app. Hard to justify for users who complete the educational content [Data]

## How to Win Against Them
- **When they say "We change behavior, not just count calories,"** respond: "Behavior change doesn't require $200/year and a class-action lawsuit. Our adaptive algorithm adjusts to your actual results — that's smarter coaching than generic daily lessons."
- **When they say "Our coaches guide you,"** respond: "Ask their users. BBB has 1,200+ complaints about billing. ConsumerAffairs reviews call coaching 'mostly automated responses.' You deserve real support, not chatbot psychology."
- **Lead with:** Transparent pricing + honest billing (1-click cancel, no dark patterns). This is Noom's biggest vulnerability.

## When They Win Over You
- Users who genuinely need structured behavioral/psychological intervention for eating disorders or emotional eating
- Enterprise/insurance-funded programs where the user doesn't pay directly
- Users seeking GLP-1 medication prescriptions integrated with a coaching program

## Their Customers' Top Complaint
"Charged $151.20 ten months after thinking they had cancelled" — BBB, 2025
This matters because: Billing malpractice destroys trust at a fundamental level. Users who feel scammed become vocal anti-advocates, and the $56M settlement confirms this is systemic, not anecdotal.

## Key Vulnerability
**Noom is pivoting away from calorie tracking.** Multiple layoff rounds cutting coaches. Resources shifting to GLP-1 medications and telehealth. The coaching product that justified premium pricing is being hollowed out. Users who stay for coaching will find less of it; users who want tracking have cheaper, better options.

## Churn Signals
- Completing educational content ("graduated" feeling) — frequency: very common
- Billing disputes — frequency: common, high severity
- Coaching quality disappointment — frequency: common
- Noom Med medication issues (expired vials, auto-renewal) — frequency: emerging

## Watch For
- IPO was planned for 2022 at ~$10B but postponed. May resurface. IPO prep could mean either investment in quality or further cost-cutting.
- GLP-1 regulatory risk: FDA scrutiny on compounded medications could impact Noom Med.
- Continued layoffs and coach reduction signals the human coaching value prop is being automated.

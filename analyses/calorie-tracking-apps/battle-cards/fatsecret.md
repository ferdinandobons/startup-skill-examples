# Battle Card: FatSecret

*Last updated: March 10, 2026*

## At a Glance
- **What they do:** Free-first calorie counter with the most generous free tier and a B2B food data API
- **Founded:** 2006 | **Funding:** Bootstrapped | **Team:** ~38
- **Price:** Free / $38.99/yr (Premium) | **Model:** Freemium + B2B API licensing
- **Best for:** Budget-conscious users who want full tracking for free, and developers needing food data

## Their Strengths (be honest)
- **Best free tier in the market** — full calorie/macro tracking, barcode scanning, and exercise tracking included free. No competitor matches this generosity [Data]
- **100M+ downloads** — massive install base driven by free tier. Highest 30-day user retention among calorie counter apps in the US [Data]
- **Hidden B2B moat** — FatSecret Platform API used by 50,000+ developers and 150+ corporate clients including Google, Samsung, Medtronic, Nestle. This creates a defensible data ecosystem [Data]
- **Lowest premium price:** $38.99/yr is the cheapest paid tier in the category [Data]

## Their Weaknesses (your openings)
- **Dated, unpolished UI** — "Interface is not as sleek or visually engaging as some other diet apps." Functional but feels like 2015 [Data — FeastGood]
- **App stability issues:** Freezing during food search, data loss (one user lost 2+ years of weight data), cross-platform sync failures [Data — JustUseApp]
- **Weight gain goal bug:** Users selecting weight gain goals still see weight loss messaging and calorie limits [Data — JustUseApp]
- **Small team (38 people)** limits feature velocity. No AI features, no adaptive coaching, no modern UX refresh [Estimate]

## How to Win Against Them
- **When they say "Best free calorie tracker,"** respond: "Free is great until the app freezes mid-logging and loses your data. We match their free features with an app that actually works reliably."
- **When they say "100M+ downloads,"** respond: "Downloads aren't users. Their retention comes from being free, not from being good. Our users stay because the product works better."
- **Lead with:** Equivalent free features + modern UI + reliability + AI logging capabilities they lack

## When They Win Over You
- Users who want zero-cost, no-premium-needed tracking with barcode scanning
- The most budget-conscious segment who won't pay any subscription
- Developers needing a food data API for their own products

## Their Customers' Top Complaint
"App freezing while searching and adding foods during meal entry, with saved items being lost" — JustUseApp, 2025
This matters because: Reliability is the minimum bar for a daily-use app. When logging food becomes a fight against the app itself, users leave — even for free products.

## Key Vulnerability
**Free but falling behind.** FatSecret's generous free tier is its moat but also its trap. Revenue (~$4-8M) can't fund the engineering needed for AI features, modern UX, and reliability improvements. While competitors ship AI photo logging and adaptive coaching, FatSecret remains stuck in 2015. The B2B API business may actually be the primary business; the consumer app may be secondary.

## Churn Signals
- Users wanting modern experience migrating to MFP, Lose It!, Yazio — frequency: common
- App stability issues (freezing, data loss) — frequency: occasional, high severity
- No AI features making it feel dated — frequency: growing
- Cross-platform sync failures — frequency: occasional

## Watch For
- Whether the B2B API business continues to sustain the consumer app, or if the app becomes neglected.
- Any major UI refresh or AI feature addition that could re-energize the user base.
- Potential acquisition by a larger player wanting the API business and 100M+ user base.

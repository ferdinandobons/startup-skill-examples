# Battle Card: MyFitnessPal

*Last updated: March 10, 2026*

## At a Glance
- **What they do:** The world's largest calorie counter and nutrition tracker with a 14M+ food database
- **Founded:** 2005 | **Funding:** PE-owned (Francisco Partners, acquired for $345M in 2020) | **Team:** ~200-400
- **Price:** Free / $79.99/yr (Premium) / $99.99/yr (Premium+) | **Model:** Feature-gated freemium
- **Best for:** Mainstream users who want the largest food database and brand recognition

## Their Strengths (be honest)
- **Largest food database in the market** (14M+ items, 68,500 brands, 380+ restaurants) — nearly impossible for a new entrant to replicate from scratch [Data]
- **200M+ registered users** create unmatched brand recognition and network effects [Data]
- **Aggressive M&A:** Acquired Cal AI (AI photo logging, $40M ARR) and Intent (meal planning) in 13 months. Plus ChatGPT Health integration. Building a nutrition super-app [Data]

## Their Weaknesses (your openings)
- **Paywalling backlash:** Barcode scanning moved behind paywall in 2022. Users call it a "pay-to-play ecosystem." This is the #1 complaint and top churn driver [Data — Trustpilot, forums]
- **March 2026 UI redesign revolt:** Long-time Premium users (10+ years) report "absolutely hate the new layout" — slow responses, removed swipe navigation, broken meal-copy. Active defection underway [Data — MFP Community Forums]
- **Database accuracy crisis:** Crowdsourced entries are riddled with errors. Users report scrolling "through 50 entries...that hasn't been updated since Obama was president" [Data — HN forums]
- **Privacy concerns:** 150M-user data breach (2018), May 2025 lawsuit alleging tracking without consent [Data]

## How to Win Against Them
- **When they say "We have the biggest database,"** respond: "Big isn't accurate. Our database is verified — every entry checked against lab-analyzed sources. MFP's 14M entries include thousands of user-submitted duplicates and errors."
- **When they say "200M users can't be wrong,"** respond: "200M signed up. Most left. Check their forums — long-time Premium users are actively switching after the March 2026 redesign."
- **Lead with:** Free barcode scanning (their biggest paywall complaint) + verified data accuracy (their biggest trust gap)

## When They Win Over You
- Users who need the absolute largest food database — especially obscure restaurant items and international branded products
- Users deeply embedded in the MFP ecosystem (years of logs, custom foods, social connections)
- Enterprise/platform integrations — MFP connects with 50+ fitness apps and devices

## Their Customers' Top Complaint
"Company is moving more features to the premium or premium plus level every day" — Trustpilot, 2025
This matters because: It signals a PE-driven monetization squeeze that prioritizes short-term revenue extraction over user loyalty, creating a growing pool of frustrated users ready to switch.

## Key Vulnerability
**The trust erosion spiral.** MFP is simultaneously paywalling features, shipping buggy redesigns, and suffering privacy incidents. Each event compounds user frustration. The March 2026 redesign is accelerating defection that was already happening. This is the best window in years to capture MFP refugees.

## Churn Signals
- Feature paywalling (barcode scanner) — frequency: very common
- UI redesign breaking established workflows — frequency: acute (March 2026)
- Database accuracy frustration — frequency: common
- Privacy/data breach concerns — frequency: occasional but high-severity

## Watch For
- Francisco Partners likely preparing for exit (acquired 2020, typical PE hold is 3-7 years). Expect continued aggressive monetization and M&A to inflate metrics before sale.
- Cal AI technology integration into main app could significantly improve AI logging experience.
- ChatGPT Health integration could create new user acquisition channel that's hard to replicate.

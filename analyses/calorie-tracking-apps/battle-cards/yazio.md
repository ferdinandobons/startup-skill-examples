# Battle Card: Yazio

*Last updated: March 10, 2026*

## At a Glance
- **What they do:** Calorie and nutrition tracker with strong intermittent fasting features, Europe-focused
- **Founded:** 2009 | **Funding:** Acquired by Groupe SEB (late 2023) | **Team:** ~144
- **Price:** Free / ~$20-50/yr (Pro, varies by region/promo) | **Model:** Freemium with aggressive discounting
- **Best for:** European users and intermittent fasting enthusiasts who want an affordable tracker

## Their Strengths (be honest)
- **Intermittent fasting market leader** — best-in-category IF features (16:8, 5:2, 6:1, 1:1 plans). Recognized as "Best for Intermittent Fasting" in 2026 roundups [Data]
- **Groupe SEB backing** — acquired by a major consumer products conglomerate, providing resources for expansion [Data]
- **Aggressive price competition** — $19.99/yr with frequent 40-75% off promotions. Among the cheapest premium options [Data]
- **Strong European presence** — operates in 150+ countries with localized content [Data]

## Their Weaknesses (your openings)
- **Deceptive billing:** "Displayed a monthly subscription price but charged users upfront for the entire year without warning." Users report charges "without consent" [Data — Sikayetvar, Apple Community]
- **Aggressive ads:** "Utterly grifty" with "tons of ads specifically designed to be as annoying as possible" [Data — Trustpilot]
- **Smaller database (4M items):** Significantly smaller than MFP (14M) or Lose It! (57M). Scanned items frequently not found [Data]
- **Limited free features:** Restricted free tier frustrates users, driving uninstalls over conversions [Data]

## How to Win Against Them
- **When they say "Best intermittent fasting tracker,"** respond: "Fasting timer is a free feature we include by default. You shouldn't need to pay $20-50/year for a countdown clock."
- **When they say "Most affordable premium,"** respond: "Affordable means nothing when they charge your card for the full year without warning. Check their billing complaints."
- **Lead with:** Transparent billing + free IF features + better database coverage

## When They Win Over You
- Users committed to structured IF programs who want dedicated fasting plan variety
- European users who find US-centric apps lacking in local food databases
- Extremely price-sensitive users in markets where Yazio prices at $10-20/yr

## Their Customers' Top Complaint
"Displayed a monthly subscription price but charged users upfront for the entire year without warning" — Sikayetvar, 2025
This matters because: Billing deception is a trust-destroying pattern. Users who feel tricked become vocal detractors and never return.

## Key Vulnerability
**Stuck between niches.** Yazio is too small to compete with MFP on database size, too basic to compete with Cronometer on accuracy, and too generic to compete with MacroFactor on intelligence. The IF niche differentiator is easily replicable — any competitor can add a fasting timer.

## Churn Signals
- Billing deception anger — frequency: common, high severity
- Ad fatigue driving uninstalls — frequency: common
- Database gaps (non-US brands not found) — frequency: occasional
- Cup of grapes listed as 350 calories (inaccuracy) — frequency: occasional but trust-eroding

## Watch For
- Groupe SEB's strategic direction — will they invest in R&D or treat Yazio as a cash cow?
- Expansion of AI features and database coverage to compete with MFP/Lose It!.
- Further aggressive discounting that could trigger a price war in the category.

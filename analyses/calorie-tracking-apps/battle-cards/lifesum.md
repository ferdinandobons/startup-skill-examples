# Battle Card: Lifesum

*Last updated: March 10, 2026*

## At a Glance
- **What they do:** Holistic digital self-care app combining calorie tracking, meal planning, diet programs, and wellness scoring
- **Founded:** 2013 | **Funding:** $55.9M raised (last round 2019) | **Team:** ~80-120
- **Price:** Free / $49.99/yr (Premium) | **Model:** Freemium with frequent 50% off promotions
- **Best for:** Health-curious users who want a lifestyle/wellness app broader than pure calorie counting

## Their Strengths (be honest)
- **65M+ users** — large installed base with strong European presence [Data]
- **Holistic wellness positioning** — "Life Score" feature, 12 diet programs, broader than pure calorie counting. Differentiated brand identity [Data]
- **Lykon acquisition (2024)** — biomarker/blood testing capabilities. Most advanced "quantified self" play alongside Cronometer [Data]
- **Beautiful design** — consistently praised for visual UI quality and user experience [Data]

## Their Weaknesses (your openings)
- **AI inaccuracy destroys trust:** "AI-like program that calculates values completely incorrectly" — premium users paying for broken AI features [Data — JustUseApp]
- **Billing nightmare:** "Cancel membership button does not work," auto-renewal charges, double-billing, unresponsive support. Multiple Trustpilot complaints [Data]
- **App-breaking bugs:** Feb 2025: users can't add foods to diary. Support chat crashes repeatedly. Feature removal after updates [Data — JustUseApp]
- **No funding since 2019** — 5+ years without new capital. Revenue (~$21M) may not sustain competitive development [Data]

## How to Win Against Them
- **When they say "More than a calorie counter — a lifestyle app,"** respond: "Lifestyle means nothing when the cancel button doesn't work and AI features calculate calories 'completely incorrectly.' Trust your tracker with one that actually works."
- **When they say "65 million users,"** respond: "65M signed up. How many stayed? Their Trustpilot is full of users who can't cancel. Downloads aren't loyalty."
- **Lead with:** Working cancel button (yes, seriously), accurate AI, and transparent billing. The bar is low.

## When They Win Over You
- Users attracted to holistic wellness approach (not just calorie counting)
- Users interested in biomarker/blood testing integration (Lykon)
- European users who prefer locally relevant diet programs and food databases

## Their Customers' Top Complaint
"Cancel membership button does not work" — Trustpilot, 2025
This matters because: A non-functional cancel button is perhaps the most damaging single UX failure possible. It transforms a product complaint into a trust violation and drives regulatory/legal risk.

## Key Vulnerability
**Funding gap + quality erosion.** No new funding since 2019, broken features, billing complaints, and AI inaccuracy paint a picture of a product in decline. The Lykon acquisition and Consupedia partnership suggest strategic ambition, but without capital or quality execution, the wellness pivot may not materialize.

## Churn Signals
- Billing/cancellation inability — frequency: common, critical severity
- AI calculation inaccuracies — frequency: common
- App-breaking bugs (can't add food, support chat crashes) — frequency: occasional, high severity
- Feature removal after updates — frequency: occasional
- Perpetual 50% off signals users don't perceive full price value — frequency: structural

## Watch For
- Whether Lykon biomarker integration materializes into a compelling product feature or remains an unfulfilled acquisition.
- Consupedia partnership (KTH Royal Institute of Technology) could add research credibility if executed well.
- Potential investor transaction or acquisition — the company has been exploring shareholder changes [Data].
- Continued quality erosion could accelerate user defection if bugs and billing issues persist.

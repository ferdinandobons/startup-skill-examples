# Battle Card: Lose It!

*Last updated: March 10, 2026*

## At a Glance
- **What they do:** User-friendly calorie tracking app focused on simplicity and weight loss goal-setting
- **Founded:** 2008 | **Funding:** $10.6M raised; acquired by Ziff Davis/Everyday Health (2022) | **Team:** ~50-100
- **Price:** Free / $39.99/yr (Premium) / $189.99 (Lifetime) | **Model:** Feature-gated freemium
- **Best for:** Beginners and cost-conscious users who want clean UX at an affordable price

## Their Strengths (be honest)
- **Best-in-class UX for beginners** — consistently praised as the simplest, most approachable calorie tracker. Clean interface reduces logging friction [Data — multiple review sites]
- **Aggressive pricing:** $39.99/yr undercuts MFP by 50%. Lifetime option ($189.99) is unique in the market [Data]
- **Large database:** 57M+ verified food items — second largest after MFP [Data]
- **Strong AI photo logging:** "Snap It" feature rated among top performers for AI food recognition accuracy [Data]

## Their Weaknesses (your openings)
- **Barcode scanner paywalled (2026):** Moved to Premium for new users — following MFP's most criticized decision [Data]
- **Maintenance mode under Ziff Davis:** No category-defining innovation. Parent company (digital media conglomerate) likely prioritizes cash flow over R&D [Assumption]
- **UI update backlash:** "Worst update or 'facelift' they've ever seen" — calendar relocated, food choices reduced by redundant button panels [Data — App Store reviews]
- **Customer service vacuum:** Described as "basically impossible" to reach [Data]

## How to Win Against Them
- **When they say "Most affordable premium tracker,"** respond: "Affordable doesn't help when they paywall barcode scanning. We include it free — because scanning a barcode shouldn't cost you $40/year."
- **When they say "Cleanest interface in the category,"** respond: "Clean until the last update broke the calendar and added redundant buttons. Our UX stays stable because we respect your muscle memory."
- **Lead with:** Free barcode scanning + stable UX that doesn't regress with updates

## When They Win Over You
- Budget-conscious users who want the cheapest premium option ($39.99/yr)
- Users who value the lifetime pricing option ($189.99 one-time)
- Complete beginners who prioritize simplicity above all else

## Their Customers' Top Complaint
"In 2026, Lose It! has moved the Barcode Scanner into the Premium feature set for most new users" — FitBudd, 2026
This matters because: They're repeating MFP's most-criticized mistake. The backlash window creates an opportunity to capture cost-conscious users who refuse to pay for barcode scanning.

## Key Vulnerability
**Ziff Davis ownership means strategic stagnation.** Ziff Davis acquires digital properties for cash flow. Lose It! will likely maintain feature parity but never lead the category. This creates a slowly widening gap as competitors innovate with AI, adaptive coaching, and GLP-1 features.

## Churn Signals
- Barcode scanner paywall (new users) — frequency: emerging, high potential
- UI update regression — frequency: common
- Custom food entry bugs (30-second timeout) — frequency: occasional, high severity
- Billing/trial conversion complaints — frequency: occasional

## Watch For
- Whether Ziff Davis invests in AI features to keep pace with MFP's Cal AI acquisition, or lets Lose It! fall behind.
- Further feature paywalling as revenue pressure increases.
- Potential acquisition by a more strategic buyer if Ziff Davis exits.

# Battle Card: Kit (formerly ConvertKit)

**Last Updated:** March 2026
**ARR:** $43M | **Growth:** 14% YoY | **Team:** ~85 people | **Funding:** Secondary only

---

## Who They Are

The "email marketing for creators" incumbent, rebranded from ConvertKit to Kit in October 2024. Targets a broader creator audience than pure newsletter platforms — bloggers, podcasters, course creators, musicians, coaches, and e-commerce sellers. Revenue primarily from flat SaaS subscriptions + 3.5% transaction fee on digital product sales. 99.5% net dollar retention — the strongest retention metric in the group. Building a Shopify-style platform with an App Store ecosystem (15+ third-party integrations).

## Their Strengths

- **Best-in-class email automations:** Industry-leading visual automation builder. Sequences, conditional logic, subscriber tagging — the deepest workflow engine in the newsletter platform space.
- **99.5% net dollar retention:** Strongest retention in the market. Once creators build automations and workflows, switching costs are very high.
- **10,000 free subscribers:** The most generous free tier. Captures creators early and converts when they need automations.
- **App Store ecosystem:** Canva, Thinkific, Circle, Gumroad, Shopify, Teachable integrations. This is a platform play, not just a newsletter tool.
- **Multi-channel creator focus:** Not just newsletters — course delivery, digital product sales, landing pages, forms. Serves the full creator economy stack.
- **Strong deliverability:** Consistently rated for high email deliverability and inbox placement.
- **Free migration assistance:** Paid plans include expert-assisted migration from other platforms.

## Their Weaknesses

- **September 2025 price shock:** Up to 4x price increase with only one month notice. Generated significant backlash: "customer service is horrendous," "only one month notice."
- **Rebrand confusion:** ConvertKit → Kit (Oct 2024) creates SEO fragmentation. Old content ranks alongside new content, confusing searchers. Brand equity built over years partially lost.
- **Slower growth (14% YoY vs. Beehiiv's 50%):** Mature SaaS trajectory, not hypergrowth. The platform play hasn't yet generated new growth vectors.
- **No ad network or marketplace:** Missing the ad monetization opportunity that Beehiiv owns.
- **Weaker discovery features:** Creator Network exists but is less developed than Substack's recommendation engine or Beehiiv's Boosts marketplace.
- **Hidden transaction fee:** 3.5% + $0.30 per transaction on commerce. Less visible than Substack's 10% but meaningful at scale.
- **UI/performance complaints:** "Slow performance, a glitchy email editor, long load times" cited in reviews.

## How to Win Against Them

**For pure newsletter creators:**
> "Kit is built for course creators, e-commerce sellers, and coaches. If you're writing a newsletter, you're paying for an email marketing platform with features you'll never use. We're purpose-built for newsletters."

**For price-sensitive creators:**
> "Kit raised prices up to 4x in September 2025 with one month notice. At 10K subscribers, you're paying ~$139/mo for the platform plus 3.5% on every transaction. We offer [comparable price] with 0% transaction fees."

**For simplicity-seekers:**
> "Kit's automation builder is powerful — if you need it. Most newsletter creators need to write, publish, and grow their list. They don't need visual workflow builders with 15 conditional branches."

**For creators wanting monetization tools:**
> "Kit takes 3.5% + $0.30 on every digital product sale. At $10K/mo in product revenue, that's ~$380/mo in hidden fees. Our platform charges $0 on your revenue."

## When They Win Over You

- When the creator needs advanced email automations (Kit's workflow builder is the best in class)
- When the creator sells digital products, courses, or memberships (Kit's commerce features are strong)
- When the creator needs deep third-party integrations (App Store with 15+ apps)
- When the creator is already in the Kit ecosystem with complex workflows built (switching cost is very high)
- When the creator wants the most generous free tier (10K subscribers free)

## Customer Objections and Responses

**"Kit has the best automations"**
> It does. If email automations are your primary need, Kit is hard to beat. But if you're primarily writing a newsletter and need growth tools (referral programs, ad network, discovery), you're paying for automation power you may not use.

**"10,000 free subscribers is amazing"**
> It is — until you need automations (the whole point of Kit). The free tier limits you to 1 automation. Serious creators upgrade quickly, and then you're on a platform optimized for course sellers, not newsletter writers.

**"They have 99.5% net dollar retention — they must be doing something right"**
> High retention can mean strong product — or high switching costs. Kit's automation workflows don't export to any other platform. Once you build complex sequences, you're locked in. That retention stat reflects lock-in as much as satisfaction.

## Key Vulnerability

**The price increase backlash + rebrand confusion.** Kit's September 2025 price hike (up to 4x) generated significant creator anger, and the ConvertKit → Kit rebrand fragmented their SEO presence. Creators searching for "ConvertKit alternatives" are actively shopping. Target this segment with migration offers and transparent pricing guarantees.

## Churn Signals

- Complaints about the September 2025 price increase
- Creators asking "is Kit still worth it?" or searching "Kit alternatives"
- Frustration with slow UI and editor performance
- Creators who don't use automations questioning why they're on Kit
- ConvertKit → Kit brand confusion in search/social
- Creators comparing Kit's 3.5% transaction fee to competitors' 0%

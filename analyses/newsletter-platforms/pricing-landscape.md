# Newsletter Platforms — Pricing Landscape Analysis

**Date:** March 2026

---

## Market Pricing Overview

- **Dominant value metrics:** Revenue share (Substack, 10%) vs. flat SaaS scaled by subscriber count (all others)
- **Price range:** $0 (all platforms offer free tiers) to ~$459/mo (Beehiiv Max at 100K) or effectively unlimited via Substack's 10% revenue share
- **Median entry paid price:** $10-49/mo for 1,000 subscribers
- **Free tier prevalence:** Universal — all 7 major platforms offer free tiers
- **Key trend:** 0% revenue share is now the competitive standard. Substack's 10% cut is the most cited pain point across all communities.

## Value Metric Analysis

### Revenue Share (Substack)
- **How it works:** Free platform, 10% of all paid subscription revenue + Stripe 2.9% + $0.30/txn
- **Why it exists:** Zero-friction entry attracts beginners. Substack only earns when creators earn — aligned incentives for early-stage writers.
- **Where it breaks:** At scale. A creator earning $100K/mo pays ~$13,500/mo to Substack + Stripe. At $50K/mo, that's $6,750/mo — more than 30x what Beehiiv charges.
- **Invisible cost escalation:** Because the 10% is deducted automatically, many creators don't internalize the cost until they hit meaningful revenue.

### Flat SaaS + 0% Share (Beehiiv, Ghost, Buttondown, MailerLite)
- **How it works:** Fixed monthly fee based on subscriber count. No cut of subscription revenue.
- **Why it wins:** Predictable costs. Scales linearly with list size, not exponentially with revenue.
- **"0% revenue share" as competitive weapon:** Beehiiv's marketing calculates exact savings vs. Substack (e.g., "at $10K/mo revenue, Substack costs you $1,000/mo — we cost $109").

### Hybrid: SaaS + Transaction Fee (Kit)
- **How it works:** Flat monthly fee + 3.5% + $0.30 per transaction on digital product/subscription sales
- **Hidden cost:** At $10K/mo in digital product sales, the transaction fee alone is ~$380/mo — on top of the platform fee.
- **Positioning risk:** Less visible than Substack's 10% but still meaningful at scale. The "0% on newsletters" messaging obscures the commerce fee.

## Pricing Psychology Breakdown

### Substack — "Free Until You're Not"
- Zero upfront cost = ultimate try-before-you-buy
- Revenue share is automatically deducted — invisible cost escalation
- Network lock-in justifies the fee: "You're paying for discovery, not just hosting"
- No contracts, no annual billing — feels low-commitment but the 10% is perpetual

### Beehiiv — "Save Money, Make Money"
- Direct Substack attack: marketing materials calculate exact savings
- Free tier (2,500 subs) hooks creators before they consider alternatives
- Feature-gating (not subscriber-gating) drives upgrades: Launch → Scale is about unlocking monetization tools, not just growing a list
- Annual discount (~15%) reduces churn

### Ghost — "Own Everything"
- Open-source credibility: self-hosting = $0 forever
- Cheapest managed entry ($18/mo)
- Sharp $29 → $199 jump is intentional: forces mid-tier creators to choose between constraints or significant investment
- 0% revenue share is marketed as ideological, not just competitive

### Kit — "The Free Funnel"
- 10,000 free subscribers = most generous free tier in market
- Feature-gating at free → Creator tier forces upgrades when creators need automations (the core value of email marketing)
- 2 months free on annual plan = standard SaaS retention play
- Transaction fee is less visible than Substack's share but adds up

### Buttondown — "Pay Only for What You Use"
- A la carte add-ons ($9-79/mo each) appeal to minimalists
- Lowest base price ($9/mo for 1K subs)
- But full-featured at 1K subs = ~$85/mo (more expensive than Beehiiv Scale at $49)
- Non-profit 50% discount signals values-driven brand
- Monthly-only billing = zero lock-in

## Pricing Whitespace

### 1. Mid-Tier Ghost Gap
Ghost jumps from Publisher ($29/mo, 1K members) to Business ($199/mo, 10K members). A $79-99/mo tier at 5K members is conspicuously absent. Creators with 2K-9K members are stranded between underpowered and overpriced.

### 2. Low Revenue Share Model
No platform offers a 3-5% revenue share between Substack's 10% and everyone else's 0%. This could appeal to creators who want zero upfront cost (like Substack) but find 10% excessive. A "graduated share" model (5% below $5K/mo, 3% above) is unexplored.

### 3. Bundled Buttondown
Buttondown's a la carte model means full-featured usage at 1K subs costs ~$85/mo — more expensive than Beehiiv Scale ($49). A discounted "everything bundle" would be competitive for the developer audience that already loves Buttondown's simplicity.

### 4. Usage-Based Pricing
No platform charges by emails sent (except Mailchimp with send limits). For seasonal or event-based newsletters, a pay-per-send model could capture a segment that doesn't want monthly subscriptions.

### 5. Enterprise Newsletter Tier
None of the creator-focused platforms (Substack, Beehiiv, Buttondown) offer enterprise-grade features (SSO, SLA, dedicated support, compliance) at scale. Only Beehiiv Enterprise and Mailchimp touch this. The $94B corporate communications market is wide open.

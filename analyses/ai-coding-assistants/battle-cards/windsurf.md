# Battle Card: Windsurf (Cognition AI / formerly Codeium)
*Last updated: March 9, 2026*

## At a Glance
- **What they do:** AI-first IDE with Cascade autonomous agent, now owned by Cognition AI (makers of Devin)
- **Founded:** 2020 (as Codeium) | **Funding:** $243M pre-acquisition; Cognition valued at $10.2B | **Team:** ~250 (integrated into Cognition)
- **Price:** $0-60/mo (credit-based) | **Model:** Credits ($0.04/credit)
- **Best for:** Developers who want an AI-native IDE at a lower price point than Cursor

## Their Strengths (be honest)
- **Lower price point.** $15/mo Pro vs. Cursor's $20/mo. Deliberately positioned as "best value." [Data]
- **Cascade agent is innovative.** Autonomous multi-file generation, recognizes helper functions, flags side effects. Won LogRocket AI Dev Tool #1 ranking (Feb 2026). [Data]
- **Combined IDE + Devin under Cognition.** Potential to merge the best editor-based and autonomous agent approaches. [Estimate]
- **Enterprise traction.** Dell, Zillow, JPMorgan Chase among customers. JPMorgan Hall of Innovation Award winner. [Data]

## Their Weaknesses (your openings)
- **Stability is a major problem.** 2 incidents in 90 days (1 major outage), tab latency issues, completions breaking, WSL crashes. [Data: incident reports, user reviews]
- **Security vulnerabilities.** 94+ known Chromium CVEs affecting 1.8M developers. Windsurf has not responded to security disclosures. [Data: security research]
- **Acquisition uncertainty.** Founders left for Google DeepMind. Remaining team absorbed by Cognition. Brand and product direction unclear. [Data: TechCrunch]
- **Broken free credits.** Trustpilot overwhelmingly 1-star reviews citing wasted credits, instability, and login issues. [Data: Trustpilot]
- **Cascade autonomy risks.** Makes changes users didn't intend or approve. Rollback workflow needs more attention than Cursor's step-by-step model. [Data: user reviews]

## How to Win Against Them
- **When they say "Cheapest AI IDE,"** respond: "A cheaper tool that crashes during long sessions costs more in lost productivity. We offer [stability + predictable pricing]."
- **When they say "Cascade handles everything autonomously,"** respond: "Autonomous doesn't mean accurate. Cascade makes unintended changes and has no self-verification. We [verify before presenting / give you control]."
- **When they say "Backed by Cognition/Devin,"** respond: "Windsurf's founders left for Google. The team was acquired, not organically built. We're [committed / founder-led / focused]."
- **Lead with:** Stability, security, and clear product direction. Windsurf's acquisition turbulence is a trust issue.

## When They Win Over You
- **Budget-conscious developers** choosing between $15/mo (Windsurf) and $20/mo (Cursor). Price matters.
- **Developers betting on the Cognition/Devin thesis** that autonomous AI agents are the future and want to be on that platform early.

## Their Customers' Top Complaint
"The experience has soured significantly due to unresolved issues." — Developer feedback, May 2025

This matters because: Windsurf promised innovation but delivered instability. Developers who've been burned are easy to convert.

## Key Vulnerability
**Brand and organizational uncertainty post-acquisition.** Founders at Google, team at Cognition, product direction unclear. Developers hate depending on a tool whose future is uncertain. Any stable alternative with comparable features wins these users.

## Churn Signals
- Crashes during long agent sessions — frequency: high
- Broken free credits system — frequency: high
- Security vulnerability concerns — frequency: growing
- Acquisition uncertainty ("will this product still exist in a year?") — frequency: medium

## Watch For
- **Cognition integration.** If Cognition successfully merges Windsurf IDE with Devin's autonomous capabilities, it could create a formidable product.
- **Pricing changes.** Post-acquisition pricing may shift. Watch for alignment with Devin's pricing model.

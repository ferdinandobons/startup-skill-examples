# Battle Card: Augment Code
*Last updated: March 9, 2026*

## At a Glance
- **What they do:** AI coding assistant with deep codebase understanding via 200K-token Context Engine, targeting teams with large codebases
- **Founded:** 2022 | **Funding:** $252M (Series B) | **Team:** ~100+
- **Price:** $20-200/mo (credit-based) | **Model:** Credits (tool-call-based)
- **Best for:** Enterprise teams working with large, complex, multi-repository codebases

## Their Strengths (be honest)
- **Best codebase understanding in the market.** 200K-token Context Engine handles 400K+ file codebases with 70.6% SWE-bench accuracy vs. 56% for file-limited competitors. [Data: benchmarks]
- **Highest code review accuracy.** Outperformed Cursor Bugbot, CodeRabbit, and others by ~10 points on public benchmarks. [Data]
- **Enterprise security certifications.** First AI coding assistant with ISO/IEC 42001 (May 2025). SOC 2 Type II. Non-extractable API. Guarantee not to train on customer code. [Data]
- **Free tier for open-source projects.** Genuinely free for OSS — builds community goodwill. [Data]

## Their Weaknesses (your openings)
- **Pricing controversy is damaging.** October 2025 switch to credit-based pricing was perceived as 10x+ price increase for heavy users. "AugmentCode New Pricing: It's Ridiculous" — Medium article title. [Data]
- **Revenue growth is lagging.** $20M ARR (October 2025) vs. Cursor's $2B. No new funding in 23 months. [Data: GetLatka]
- **Limited IDE support.** Primarily VS Code. JetBrains users, Vim users locked out. [Data: user complaints]
- **Credit variance is extreme.** 293 to 4,261 credits per task. Auto top-up at $15/24K creates unpredictable bills. Heavy users report $300-600/mo actual costs. [Data: user reports]
- **Low brand awareness.** Not a default in "best AI coding tools" listicles. Most organic content is Augment's own marketing. [Data: SEO analysis]

## How to Win Against Them
- **When they say "We understand your entire codebase,"** respond: "At what price? Heavy Augment users pay $300-600/mo. We deliver [comparable context / whole-repo understanding] at [fraction of the cost]."
- **When they say "SOC 2 + ISO 42001,"** respond: "Great certifications. We also have [equivalent security] plus [predictable pricing / broader IDE support]."
- **When they say "70.6% SWE-bench accuracy,"** respond: "Benchmarks don't reflect real-world usage with 4,261 credits per complex task. Our accuracy + [self-verification] means you trust the first result."
- **Lead with:** Same codebase depth, predictable pricing, broader IDE support.

## When They Win Over You
- **Enterprise teams with 100K+ file monorepos** where Context Engine's depth is unmatched.
- **Security-first enterprises** that require ISO 42001 + SOC 2 Type II certifications.
- **Teams already invested in Augment's Context Engine** where switching means losing learned patterns.

## Their Customers' Top Complaint
"AugmentCode New Pricing: It's Ridiculous" — Medium article title, 2025

This matters because: A 67% price increase with opaque credit consumption destroys trust with the developers who advocated for Augment internally.

## Key Vulnerability
**Falling behind on growth while burning pricing trust.** $20M ARR vs. $2B (Cursor) and $1B+ (Copilot) after raising $252M. No new funding in 23 months. If enterprise conversion doesn't accelerate, Augment risks becoming an acquisition target.

## Churn Signals
- Pricing controversy driving evaluation of alternatives — frequency: medium
- VS Code-only limitation losing JetBrains developers — frequency: medium
- Credit-based pricing creating unpredictability — frequency: medium
- Low brand awareness vs. Copilot/Cursor — frequency: ongoing

## Watch For
- **New funding round.** 23 months without raising is notable. Either capital-efficient or struggling to raise at higher valuation.
- **IDE expansion.** JetBrains support would significantly expand addressable market.
- **Acquisition.** If growth stalls, Augment's Context Engine technology could attract acquirers (Microsoft, Amazon, JetBrains).

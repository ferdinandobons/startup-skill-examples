# Battle Card: Sourcegraph Cody / Amp
*Last updated: March 9, 2026*

## At a Glance
- **What they do:** AI coding assistant built on code search infrastructure, now splitting into Cody (enterprise search) and Amp (agentic agent)
- **Founded:** 2013 (Sourcegraph) | **Funding:** $225M+ (peaked at $2.6B valuation) | **Team:** ~157 (shrinking)
- **Price:** $59/user/mo (Cody Enterprise); Amp credit-based | **Model:** Per-seat (Cody) / credits (Amp)
- **Best for:** Large enterprises with massive codebases needing cross-repository search and comprehension

## Their Strengths (be honest)
- **Superior large codebase understanding.** RAG-based architecture with up to 1M-token context. Multi-repo code search is unmatched. [Data]
- **Enterprise pedigree.** 4/6 top US banks, 15+ government agencies, Fortune 500 customers (Palo Alto Networks, Qualtrics). [Data]
- **Code search heritage.** A decade of code intelligence infrastructure. No competitor has this depth. [Data]
- **Gartner "sole Visionary."** Industry analyst recognition, though notably not "Leader." [Data]

## Their Weaknesses (your openings)
- **Plan discontinuation alienated developers.** Free, Pro, and Enterprise Starter plans all discontinued July 2025. Only Cody Enterprise remains. Individual developers are locked out. [Data]
- **Fragmentation.** Splitting into Sourcegraph + Amp as independent companies creates brand confusion and organizational uncertainty. [Data]
- **Talent drain.** "Opt-in severance" (disguised layoffs), 157 employees remaining, key departures. [Data]
- **Not a daily driver.** Excels at searching and understanding large codebases but not at the core edit-code-test loop most developers live in. [Data: user reviews]
- **Premium pricing with shrinking value.** $59/user/mo for enterprise-only access in a market where Copilot is $19/user and Cursor is $40/user. [Data]

## How to Win Against Them
- **When they say "We understand the largest codebases,"** respond: "Understanding is only half the battle. We [understand your codebase AND write accurate code / execute tasks]. Cody searches; we build."
- **When they say "Fortune 500 trust us,"** respond: "Fortune 500 trusted Cody — which they then discontinued for non-enterprise users, fragmented into two companies, and lost key talent. We're [stable / growing / committed]."
- **Lead with:** Complete, stable product vs. fragmented, uncertain future. Available to all developers, not just enterprises.

## When They Win Over You
- **Enterprises with 10M+ LOC codebases** where Sourcegraph's code search infrastructure is already deployed.
- **Teams that need code search + AI in one platform.** The Sourcegraph bundle (search + batch changes + insights + AI) is unique.

## Their Customers' Top Complaint
"Cody Free and Pro were discontinued with little warning. Individual developers have no path forward." — Community feedback, July 2025

This matters because: Abandoning individual developers kills the bottom-up adoption pipeline that enterprise tools need.

## Key Vulnerability
**Organizational instability.** Multiple pivots, plan discontinuations, company fragmentation, and talent drain signal a company in strategic crisis. Enterprise buyers evaluating Sourcegraph/Cody/Amp face legitimate questions about long-term viability.

## Churn Signals
- Individual plan discontinuation forcing users elsewhere — frequency: already happened
- Brand confusion (Sourcegraph vs. Cody vs. Amp) — frequency: ongoing
- "Not a daily driver" perception — frequency: common
- Gartner "Visionary" not "Leader" raising procurement concerns — frequency: medium

## Watch For
- **Amp's independent trajectory.** If Amp achieves product-market fit as an agentic tool, it could revive Sourcegraph's relevance.
- **Potential acquisition.** $2.6B peak valuation, now likely sub-$1B. Attractive IP and enterprise relationships for a larger acquirer.
